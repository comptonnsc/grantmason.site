About the Author

Grant Mason writes about family, identity, class, resilience, belonging, memory, and the distance between public appearances and private realities. Raised in Kentucky horse country and shaped by the contradictions of Southern tradition, privilege, collapse and reinvention, his work examines what families teach in silence and what a person must eventually learn alone.

Now connected to Charleston, South Carolina, Mason brings a reflective Southern perspective to literary nonfiction. His debut memoir Seersucker & Silence explores inheritance, loss, personal growth, and the long process of building a life beyond the mythology of where one came from.

Sample Chapter

A taste of the memoir’s voice.

If you grew up where I grew up, the fences were never just fences. They were the clean white lines around what people wanted seen, the boundaries that made order look natural. Behind them, horses moved like money made graceful. Houses sat far enough from the road to suggest privacy and close enough to remind you that privacy was also a form of display.
