About the author

Grant Mason

Grant Mason is a Southern memoirist and writer whose work explores family, identity, class, belonging, memory, and the distance between public appearances and private realities.

Raised in Kentucky horse country and shaped by a world of tradition, performance, and sudden loss, Mason writes literary nonfiction rooted in lived experience. His debut memoir, Seersucker & Silence, examines privilege, family expectations, resilience, and the long work of becoming a person apart from what one inherits.

Now connected to Charleston, South Carolina, Mason’s work carries the contrast between inland horse country and coastal reinvention, between inherited silence and the courage required to speak plainly.

Opening excerpt

Read a sample.

If you grew up in the kind of Kentucky people imagine from a distance, you learned early that beauty could become a form of instruction. White fences did not simply mark property. They taught order. Horse farms did not merely hold horses. They held a version of life everyone agreed to admire. Even silence had polish there. It could sit beside you at dinner, walk through a country club dining room, or ride home in the back seat of a Mercedes without ever announcing itself.

I did not know then that a family could look complete from the outside while its private language was already failing. I only knew that certain things were not discussed, certain questions were redirected, and certain disappointments were handled with posture. This book begins in that space between what was visible and what was true.
